--- layout: project title: "Inch Worm" ---
  • Microbit
  • Cardboard strip
  • Servo
  • Three jumper wires
  • Three wires with crocodile clips
  • Metal wire
  • Tape
  • Googley eyes
  • Coins for weight

Fold cardboard in half lengthways.

Cut triangles on both the short edges to make teeth.

Fold over 1cm of cardboard on each side to make feet.

Fold over 1cm of cardboard on each side backwards to make feet.

Hold motor upright and turn arm as far anticlockwise as it will go

Remove the arm and replace it at the Nine o'clock position

Tape the servo to the back of the cardboard. Half way up the side with the arm pointing out from the side.

Take ten centimeters of wire bend a right angle a thrid of the way along to make an 'L'

Push the longer end through the hole nearest the end of the arm on your motor and bend the end over to hold it in place.

Tape the other end of the wire to the front of your Inch Worm, about half way up.

Decorate your Inch worm.

Push the ends of the jumper wires into the plug on the motor wires.

Use some tape to fix them into place

Write code

Add an on button A pressed.

Put a servo write pin block (180)

Add image to show the worm's legs are closed.

Duplicate for B 0 degrees

on start set to 0 and add image

Download code onto microbit

Connect crocodile clips to the three wires from your Inch worm

Connect the clip connected to the orange wire from the servo to pin zero

Connect the clip connected to the red wire from the servo to 3v

Connect the clip connected to the brown wire from the servo to gnd

Plug battery in and test your Inch worm.

Doe syour Inch worm have trouble moving forward?

Try adding more weight on the inside of the front face

Does the worm not close tight enough, bend the wire to shorten it's stride

Race!
